Awards

Some blurp about recognition of exeplary efforts.

Each year, Section 4 recognizes and rewards exemplary efforts made by both individuals and chapters to further the ideals of Alpha Phi Omega's cardinal principles of Leadership, Friendship, and Service. This recognition includes notable efforts contributed at the sectional level by individuals, whether they be alumnus or current students.

Award Types

Seven awards are offered for individuals. They include the following:

  • Sectional Distinguished Service Key - Given in recognition to members for outstanding service to the fraternity. The Distinguished Service Key is the highest distinction the section may bestow upon an individual.
  • Blake R. Boer Distinguished Leadership Award - Awarded in recognition of exceptional dedication and contribution to the Leadership programs of Section 4.
  • Chuck Cruz Distinguished Fellowship Award - Awarded in recognition of exceptional dedication and contribution to the Fellowship programs of Section 4.
  • Joel Holmes and Daniel Updegraff Distinguished Service Award - Awarded in recognition of exceptional dedication and contribution to the Service programs of Section 4.
  • David A. Emery Distinguished Alumni Award - Awarded to an Alumnus in recognition of exceptional dedication and contribution to the Leadership, Fellowship, or Service programs of Section 4.
  • Bright Spot Award - Awarded to individuals who have done something special at the sectional level.
  • Ocho Award - Awarded to any active brother who visits 8 or more different chapters within one year, measured from one Spring Sectionals to the next.

Five awards are utilized to distinguish outstanding efforts put forth by chapters:

  • Gold Pan - Partitioned in to the four categories of Leadership, Fellowship, Service and Overall. The Gold Pans are awarded to chapters to distinguish exceptional efforts put forth in one of the three programs, or their approach to their overall program.
  • Ernie Manfredini Chapter Resilience Award - Awarded to a chapter that has exhibited a particular resilience and strength in the face of substantial hardship.
  • Man-Mile Award - Awarded to the chapter that has traveled the greatest distance to attend a conference, as a function of distance traveled multiplied by number of members.
